Hi, I hope you like my website.

Dmitri Volkov’s work in music and computer science has been described as “interesting,” whatever that may mean. As an award-winning composer, Dmitri aims to develop and apply cutting-edge experimental techniques, bringing forward the boundaries of music. He is also an accomplished violinist and multi-instrumentalist. Active in both theoretical and practical areas of computer science, Dmitri has presented at conferences and contributed to textbooks, and also released successful commercial apps and plugins.

Dmitri’s music composition awards include: NYC Contemporary Music Symposium finalist, RIOCC Organ Composition Competition Young Artist Division 1st Prize, ASCAP Morton Gould Young Composer Competition finalist. His computer music work has been presented at the International Symposium for Quantum Computer Music, the Tui St. George Tucker Symposium, and the MOXsonic, SPLICE, and REF Resilience music festivals. Dmitri is a co-author of the first chapter of Advances in Quantum Computer Music, published by World Scientific. He develops Pivotuner, an audio plugin enabling adaptive pure intonation for virtual instruments, alongside other apps and plugins. Dmitri also wants you to know that he does not usually write in the third person.
